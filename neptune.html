Neptune

Neptune, the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun in our solar system, is a mysterious and captivating world. Known for its striking blue color due to the presence of methane in its atmosphere, Neptune is a cold, dark, and windy planet. Its atmosphere is primarily composed of hydrogen, helium, and methane, creating fierce storms and strong winds that can reach up to 2,100 km/h (1,300 mph). Neptune also has a series of faint rings and an impressive array of 14 known moons, with Triton being the largest and most interesting.

  • Diameter: 49,244 km (30,598 miles)
  • Distance from the Sun: 4.5 billion km (2.8 billion miles)
  • Orbital Period: 165 Earth years
  • Number of Moons: 14
  • Atmospheric Composition: Hydrogen, Helium, Methane
  • Unique Feature: Great Dark Spot, a storm comparable to Jupiter's Great Red Spot
  • Notable Moon: Triton, known for its retrograde orbit and geysers
