Venus

Venus, the second planet from the Sun, is often referred to as Earth's sister planet due to their similar size and composition. However, Venus has a thick, toxic atmosphere composed mostly of carbon dioxide, with clouds of sulfuric acid, trapping heat and making it the hottest planet in our solar system. Surface temperatures on Venus can reach up to 465°C (869°F). The planet's surface is marked by vast plains, highland regions, and numerous volcanoes, making it a fascinating world of contrasts.

  • Diameter: 12,104 km (7,521 miles)
  • Distance from the Sun: 108.2 million km (67.2 million miles)
  • Orbital Period: 225 Earth days
  • Number of Moons: None
  • Atmospheric Composition: 96.5% Carbon Dioxide, 3.5% Nitrogen, traces of Sulfuric Acid
  • Surface Features: Volcanoes, vast plains, and highland regions
  • Unique Feature: Hottest planet in the solar system with a runaway greenhouse effect
