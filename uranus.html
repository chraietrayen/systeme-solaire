Uranus

Uranus, the seventh planet from the Sun, is distinct for its extreme axial tilt, causing it to rotate on its side. This unique feature results in unusual seasonal variations. Uranus has a cold atmosphere mainly composed of hydrogen and helium, with traces of methane that give the planet its characteristic blue-green hue. The planet is also known for its faint ring system and a collection of 27 moons, with Titania and Oberon being the largest and most well-known.

  • Diameter: 50,724 km (31,518 miles)
  • Distance from the Sun: 2.87 billion km (1.78 billion miles)
  • Orbital Period: 84 Earth years
  • Number of Moons: 27
  • Atmospheric Composition: Hydrogen, Helium, Methane
  • Unique Feature: Extreme axial tilt causing it to rotate on its side
  • Notable Moons: Titania and Oberon
