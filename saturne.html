Saturn

Saturn, the sixth planet from the Sun, is renowned for its stunning and extensive ring system, which is the most complex and prominent in our solar system. This gas giant has an atmosphere primarily composed of hydrogen and helium. Saturn is notable not only for its rings but also for its large number of moons, totaling 82. Among these moons, Titan stands out as the second-largest moon in the solar system, boasting a thick atmosphere and liquid methane lakes on its surface.

  • Diameter: 116,460 km (72,366 miles)
  • Distance from the Sun: 1.43 billion km (888 million miles)
  • Orbital Period: 29.5 Earth years
  • Number of Moons: 82
  • Atmospheric Composition: Hydrogen, Helium
  • Unique Feature: Extensive and complex ring system
  • Notable Moon: Titan, known for its thick atmosphere and liquid methane lakes
